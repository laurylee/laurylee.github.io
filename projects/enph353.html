Simulated Parking Enforcing Robot

UBC ENPH 353 | 2022

Overview

As a team of of two, my partner and I developed a self-navigating car in a Gazebo simulation. While my partner employed PID and Q-learning control algorithms to navigate various terrains and moving objects, I employed classical machine vision techniques and convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to identify then read license plates achieving 92% prediction accuracy. By using ROS and its topics feature in Python, we could then share data between the robot's various systems including the main navigation controller and the license plate recognition module.

Lauryn Lee 2025
